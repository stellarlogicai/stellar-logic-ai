## **TAX CALCULATION METHODS**

### **Self-Employment Tax Calculation**
**Formula:**
- Net Business Income × 92.35% = SE Taxable Income
- SE Taxable Income × 15.3% = Self-Employment Tax
- Above-the-line deduction: 50% of SE Tax

**Example:**
- Net Business Income: $100,000
- SE Taxable Income: $92,350
- Self-Employment Tax: $14,129
- Tax Deduction: $7,065

---

### **Federal Income Tax Calculation**
**Tax Brackets (2024 Single Filer):**
- 10% on income up to $11,000
- 12% on income $11,001 to $44,725
- 22% on income $44,726 to $95,375
- 24% on income $95,376 to $182,050
- 32% on income $182,051 to $231,250
- 35% on income $231,251 to $578,125
- 37% on income over $578,125

**Standard Deduction:** $13,850 (2024 Single)

---

### **Quarterly Estimated Tax Formula**
**Safe Harbor Methods:**
1. **100% of Previous Year Tax:** Pay 25% each quarter
2. **90% of Current Year Tax:** Estimate and pay quarterly
3. **Annualized Income Method:** For seasonal income

**Recommended:** 110% of previous year tax for high-income earners

## **TAX DEDUCTION OPTIMIZATION**

### **Business Expense Deductions**
**Fully Deductible:**
- Software subscriptions and technology tools
- Home office expenses (business portion)
- Business insurance premiums
- Professional development and training
- Marketing and advertising costs
- Office supplies and equipment
- Legal and professional services

**Partially Deductible:**
- Meals and entertainment (50% deductible)
- Vehicle expenses (business mileage)
- Health insurance premiums (self-employed)
- Retirement contributions (with limits)
- Home mortgage interest (home office portion)

---

### **Home Office Deduction Options**
**Simplified Method:**
- $5 per square foot up to 300 square feet
- Maximum deduction: $1,500
- No complex calculations required
- Cannot claim depreciation

**Regular Method:**
- Calculate actual home office expenses
- Include mortgage interest, utilities, insurance
- Depreciation on home portion
- More complex but potentially higher deduction

**Documentation Required:**
- Office square footage measurement
- Total home square footage
- Expense records and receipts
- Business use justification

---

### **Retirement Account Contributions**
**SEP IRA:**
- Contribution limit: 25% of net self-employment income
- Maximum: $66,000 (2024)
- Tax-deductible contribution
- Flexible contribution timing

**Solo 401(k):**
- Employee contribution: $22,500 (2024)
- Employer contribution: 25% of net income
- Maximum total: $66,000 (2024)
- Tax-deductible contributions

**Traditional IRA:**
- Contribution limit: $6,500 (2024)
- Income phase-out applies
- Tax-deductible based on income
- Additional catch-up contributions if 50+

### **Record Retention Requirements**
**IRS Guidelines:**
- **3 years** for standard tax returns
- **7 years** for business asset depreciation
- **6 years** for substantial underreporting
- **Indefinite** for fraud or no return filed

**Recommended Practice:**
- Keep all business records for 7 years
- Digital backup with multiple locations
- Organized by tax year and category
- Secure storage with access controls
